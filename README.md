## What Is Escrow Protection?


Escrow protection is a transaction method designed to reduce fraud between buyers and vendors. In a typical dark&#8195;net market setup that uses escrow:


1. **The buyer sends funds to a third-party account** (controlled by the marketplace, not the seller).
2. **The vendor ships the product or fulfills the service** after confirming payment.
3. **Only after the buyer confirms receipt and satisfaction** does the marketplace release the funds to the vendor.


This system protects both parties: buyers avoid scams where they pay but receive nothing, and vendors are safeguarded against chargebacks or false claims—since cryptocurrency payments are irreversible.


## Why Escrow Matters in dark&#8195;net Transactions


Markets that operate without escrow (often called **direct-deal** or **multisig** models) carry significantly higher risk. Without a trusted intermediary holding the funds, either party can more easily defraud the other. However, even escrow-protected markets are not foolproof. Exit scams—where a marketplace disappears with users’ funds—have occurred repeatedly in dark&#8195;net history. Due to the anonymous and unregulated nature of these platforms, risk remains inherent.
